Frequently Asked Questions

What is MDPP?
The Medicare Diabetes Prevention Program helps people with prediabetes learn healthy habits to reduce their risk of type 2 diabetes.

Is it really free?
Yes. MDPP is a covered preventive service under Medicare Part B with no cost-sharing.

Can I do this from home?
Absolutely. All sessions are delivered through the app on your phone — complete them anytime, at your own pace.

I did a diabetes prevention program before. Can I do it again?
Yes! As of February 2026, the once-per-lifetime limit has been removed. You can enroll again.

You don't currently qualify for MDPP

Based on your health records, you may not meet the clinical criteria for the Medicare Diabetes Prevention Program at this time. This could be because:

  • Your A1C is in the normal range (below 5.7%)
  • Your A1C indicates diabetes (6.5% or higher)
  • A Type 2 diabetes diagnosis was found in your records
  • Your most recent lab results are older than 12 months

If your A1C indicates diabetes:

We offer diabetes management programs that may help. Talk to your doctor about your options.

If your lab results are too old:

Medicare covers diabetes screenings — ask your doctor for a new HbA1c test, then come back to check your eligibility.

Your health still matters

Even though you don't qualify for this program right now, there's a lot you can do. Talk to your doctor about your results and what steps make sense for you. Your eligibility may change over time — you're always welcome to check again.

This program isn't available to you right now

The Medicare Diabetes Prevention Program has specific eligibility requirements, and it looks like they aren't met at this time. That doesn't mean your health isn't important — it absolutely is.

Here's what you can do

There are still good options to stay on top of your health:

  • 👩‍⚕️ Talk to your doctor — Ask about your diabetes risk and what preventive steps make sense for you
  • 🔬 Get a blood test — Medicare covers diabetes screenings at no cost to you. A simple A1C test can show where you stand
  • 📞 Call 1-800-MEDICARE — If you have questions about your Medicare coverage, they're there to help

💡 Your eligibility may change

Many people become eligible after getting a new blood test, updating their Medicare coverage, or as their health changes. You're always welcome to come back and check again.

❤️ Remember: The fact that you looked into this program tells us something important — you're taking your health seriously. Keep it up. Small, everyday choices add up to big changes over time.

If you believe you received this message in error, or if your health situation has recently changed, please don't hesitate to check your eligibility again or speak with your doctor.
